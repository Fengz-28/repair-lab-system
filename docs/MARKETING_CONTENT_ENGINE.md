# RepairLab - Marketing Content Engine

## Purpose

This document explains how RepairLab can support marketing and content generation for the workshop using real repair work.

The content engine is draft-first. Nothing should be published automatically without human approval.

## Goals

- Turn completed repairs into useful content drafts.
- Explain services clearly to local customers.
- Build trust with proof of real workshop work.
- Support Google Business, Instagram, Facebook, TikTok, and blog content.
- Educate customers without exposing private information.

## Data Sources

Potential sources from RepairLab:

- Ticket title or repair code.
- Device type.
- Brand/model when safe.
- Reported issue.
- Diagnosis.
- Repair actions.
- Parts used.
- Time to repair, if useful and accurate.
- Before/after photos if available and approved.
- Customer-approved case notes.
- Quote/invoice category, without exposing customer financial details.

Do not use:

- Customer name.
- Phone number.
- Email.
- Address.
- Serial number.
- Internal notes.
- Audit logs.
- Private photos without approval.
- Payment details.
- Sensitive device data.

## Content Outputs

RepairLab can help draft:

- Instagram post.
- Facebook post.
- TikTok short script.
- Google Business update.
- Blog post idea.
- Educational repair tip.
- Before/after caption.
- Service explanation.
- FAQ answer.
- Customer message template.

## Draft Types

### Before/After Caption

Use when:

- Photos are approved.
- The repair result is visually clear.
- No private customer/device data is visible.

### Educational Tip

Use when:

- The repair teaches customers how to prevent damage.
- The issue is common.
- The explanation can be simple and useful.

### Service Explanation

Use when:

- The workshop wants to promote a service.
- Customers often misunderstand the repair.
- The service has good margin or demand.

### Short Video Script

Use when:

- The repair has a clear visual process.
- The content can be explained in 15 to 45 seconds.
- No private details are shown.

### Google Business Update

Use when:

- The shop wants local trust.
- The service is available now.
- The post is clear, short, and practical.

## Safety Rules

- Never expose personal customer data.
- Never publish automatically without human approval.
- Never show private device information.
- Never show serial numbers.
- Never use private photos without approval.
- Never make unrealistic repair promises.
- Never imply every device can be repaired.
- Never present estimated repair time as a guarantee.
- Keep technical explanations understandable.
- Keep tone professional and trustworthy.

## Quality Rules

Good content should:

- Be based on real workshop capability.
- Explain the customer problem.
- Show the repair value.
- Include a simple call to action.
- Avoid exaggerated claims.
- Be understandable to non-technical customers.
- Reinforce trust and professionalism.

## Example Content Flow

```txt
Ticket delivered
  -> Check if repair is content-worthy
  -> Remove sensitive data
  -> Select channel
  -> Generate draft
  -> Human review
  -> Edit if needed
  -> Publish manually
  -> Track result if possible
```

## Content Review Checklist

Before publishing:

- Customer data removed?
- Serial numbers removed?
- Photos approved?
- No private notes included?
- Claim is accurate?
- Tone is professional?
- Call to action is clear?
- Service is actually offered?
- Warranty/guarantee language is safe?

## Future Automation

Future automation may suggest content drafts automatically after a ticket is delivered.

This should only happen if:

- The content remains draft-only.
- Sensitive fields are filtered.
- Photos require explicit approval.
- A human reviews before publishing.
- The workshop can disable suggestions.

Automatic publishing is out of scope until explicitly approved.
